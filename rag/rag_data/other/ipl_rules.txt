
Powerplay:

The IPL Powerplay is the first six overs of a batting innings where strict fielding restrictions apply. Only a maximum of two fielders are allowed outside the 30-yard circle.The remaining 9 fielders must be positioned as follows:Inside the circle: A maximum of 5 fielders outside the circle for the remaining overs do not apply here, as the outer ring is defined strictly by the two-fielder limit.Close catching positions: In the first two overs of the powerplay, at least two stationary fielders (excluding the wicketkeeper) must be positioned in close catching positions to take early wickets.Off-side/Leg-side limits: No more than 5 fielders are permitted on the leg side at any given time.Why It MattersThese strict fielding restrictions heavily favor the batting team, giving them a golden opportunity to hit boundaries over the infield. Teams usually deploy their most aggressive opening batters during this phase to maximize their run rate.Notable Powerplay RecordsHighest IPL Powerplay Score: Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH) smashed an astonishing 125/0 against the Delhi Capitals in 2024.Top 3 Highest Team Totals:125/0 – SRH vs DC (2024)116/0 – Punjab Kings (PBKS) vs DC (2026)107/0 – SRH vs Lucknow Super Giants (LSG) (2024)


Impact player:

The Impact Player rule in the IPL allows teams to substitute one player from their starting XI with a designated player from their five named substitutes, who can then fully participate by batting, bowling, or fielding. The replaced player takes no further part in the match.Key Aspects of the RuleSubstitution Timing: The swap can occur at the start of an innings, when a wicket falls, at the end of an over, or when a batter retires.Usage: Each team is allowed only one substitution per game.Overseas Limit: A team can only bring in an overseas player as an Impact Player if they currently have three or fewer foreigners in the field, keeping to the four-overseas player maximum.Bowling Allowance: An incoming player can bowl their full quota of four overs, regardless of whether the player they replaced has already bowled.Impact on the GameIntroduced to boost tactical flexibility, this rule effectively allows for a 12-player game, often used to strengthen the batting lineup when chasing or to add an extra bowler. While enhancing game dynamics and entertainment, it has faced criticism from some experts for reducing the role of all-rounders

Strategic time out:

A strategic timeout in the IPL is a mandatory 2-minute and 30-second break during each innings used for tactical planning and commercial broadcasting. Each team gets one timeout per innings, providing an opportunity to regroup, alter tactics, or disrupt the opposition's momentum.How the Strategic Timeout WorksDuration: Each timeout lasts exactly 2 minutes and 30 seconds.Bowling Team Timeout: The fielding team can call their timeout between the conclusion of the 6th and 9th overs.Batting Team Timeout: The batting team can opt to take theirs between the end of the 13th and 16th overs.Automatic Call: If the teams do not call for their timeouts by the end of the 9th and 16th overs, the on-field umpires will automatically signal for them.Why the Strategic Timeout ExistsTactical Planning: It allows captains and coaches to come onto the field to adjust strategies, discuss bowling changes, set field placements, or re-evaluate batting targets.Commercial Value: The breaks are highly lucrative for the league and broadcasters, providing guaranteed slots for commercials and sponsor visibility (famously sponsored by CEAT).Player Rest: It allows the players to hydrate and catch their breath during the fast-paced Twenty20 matches.


DRS:
DRS in the IPL stands for Decision Review System. It is a technology-based mechanism that allows players to challenge an on-field umpire’s decision or request a review of wide and no-ball calls.Key RulesQuota: Each team is allowed two reviews per innings.Reviewable Decisions: Teams can review dismissals (like caught-behind or LBW), as well as on-field decisions regarding wides and no-balls.Time Limit: A team has 15 seconds from the moment the umpire makes the decision to request a review.Retaining Reviews: If a challenge successfully overturns the on-field umpire's decision, the team retains their review. If the decision stands, or if it is an "Umpire's Call" (where the technology is inconclusive), the review is lost.Technologies UsedWhen a review is triggered, the third umpire uses a combination of advanced systems to make a definitive ruling:UltraEdge/Snicko: Uses sound and visual waveforms to determine if the bat made contact with the ball.Ball-Tracking (Hawk-Eye): Projects the ball's trajectory for LBW appeals to see if it would have hit the stumps.Umpire's CallIn borderline LBW decisions, the Umpire's Call rule applies. If the on-field umpire rules a batsman "not out" and ball-tracking shows the ball hitting the stumps, but only clips the edge, the decision stands because the margin is within the umpire's original margin of error


Super Over:
A Super Over in the IPL is a tie-breaker used to determine the winner of a match when both teams finish with the exact same number of runs after their regular 20 overs. It is essentially a thrilling "one-over shootout" where each team plays a single 6-ball over, and the team that scores the most runs wins.Here is exactly how it works in the IPL:The Rules of the Super OverPlayer Selection: Each team nominates just three batsmen and one bowler to play the over.Wickets: If the batting team loses 2 wickets before the 6 balls are bowled, their over ends immediately.Batting Order: The team that batted second in the main match gets to bat first in the Super Over.Player Restrictions: A bowler cannot bowl in back-to-back Super Overs, and a batsman who is dismissed in the first Super Over is not allowed to bat in any subsequent ones.Statistics: The runs scored and wickets taken during the Super Over do not count toward a player's official career statistics.What if the Super Over is tied?If both teams score the exact same number of runs in their Super Over, another Super Over (a Double Super Over) is played until a winner is declared. In the past, if there was a tie, the winner was determined by which team hit more boundaries during the main match, but this rule was updated for playoff and knockout situations to ensure a fairer, on-field conclusion
