The Orange Cap is an annual award presented to the leading run-scorer in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Introduced on April 25, 2008, it is worn by the top run-getter during matches and officially awarded at the conclusion of the tournament.

Key Records & Trivia
Most Wins: Australian batter David Warner holds the record for winning the Orange Cap the most times, claiming it in 2015, 2017, and 2019.
Highest Single-Season Run Tally: Virat Kohli (2016) holds the record for the most runs scored in a single IPL edition with an incredible 973 runs.
Back-to-Back Wins: Chris Gayle is the only player in IPL history to win the Orange Cap in consecutive seasons (2011 and 2012).
Youngest Winner: Rajasthan Royals' prodigy Vaibhav Sooryavanshi shattered the record in 2026, becoming the youngest player to ever win the Orange Cap at just 15 years old, scoring 776 runs.
Team Success: While winning the Orange Cap indicates a great individual tournament, only two players—Robin Uthappa (2014) and Ruturaj Gaikwad (2021)—have managed to win both the Orange Cap and the IPL trophy in the exact same year
