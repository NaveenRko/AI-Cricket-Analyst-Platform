In cricket, a captain is a player who leads the team and has additional roles and responsibilities.[1][2] The Indian Premier League (IPL) is a professional league for Twenty20 cricket in India,[3] which has been held annually since its first edition in 2008.[4][5]

In the 19 seasons played till 2026, 75 players have captained their team in at least one match.[6] Mumbai Indians' Rohit Sharma and Chennai Super Kings's MS Dhoni are the most successful captains with five IPL titles each.[7][8] Dhoni also holds the records for most matches (229) and most wins (134) as a captain.[9][6] Shreyas Iyer, Mahela Jayawardene, Ajinkya Rahane, Kumar Sangakkara, and Steven Smith are the only players to have captained three different teams,[10][6] with Shreyas Iyer being the only captain to have led three different sides to the finals of the IPL.[11] Kolkata Knight Riders is the only team to have won IPL with different captains


Most Successful Captains (By Trophies) :
MS Dhoni: 5 titles with Chennai Super Kings (2010, 2011, 2018, 2021, 2023).
Rohit Sharma: 5 titles with Mumbai Indians (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, 2020).
Gautam Gambhir: 2 titles with Kolkata Knight Riders (2012, 2014).

Other Winning Captains
Several other leaders have successfully guided their franchises to an IPL championship:
Shane Warne: 1 title with Rajasthan Royals (2008).
Adam Gilchrist: 1 title with Deccan Chargers (2009).
David Warner: 1 title with Sunrisers Hyderabad (2016).
Hardik Pandya: 1 title with Gujarat Titans (2022).
Shreyas Iyer: 1 title with Kolkata Knight Riders (2024).

All-Time Captaincy RecordsMost Matches: MS Dhoni has led in 235 IPL matches.
Most Wins: MS Dhoni leads with the most wins (over 130) and the highest win percentage among captains with 100+ matches.
Most Finals: MS Dhoni holds the record for reaching the most finals as a captain
