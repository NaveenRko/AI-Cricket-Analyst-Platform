The record for the fastest century in IPL history is held by Chris Gayle, who reached the milestone in just 30 balls while playing for Royal Challengers Bangalore against Pune Warriors India in 2013.
The top fastest centuries in IPL history include the following record-breaking performances:

1. Chris Gayle | 30 Balls
Team: Royal Challengers Bangalore
Opponent: Pune Warriors India
Year: 2013
Details: The most devastating innings in T20 history. Gayle reached his 100 off just 30 balls and went on to score a monumental 175 not out, which remains the highest individual score in all of T20 cricket. The innings included an astonishing 17 sixes and 13 fours, helping RCB post a mammoth total of 263/5.

2. Vaibhav Suryavanshi | 35 Balls
Team: Rajasthan Royals
Opponent: Gujarat Titans
Year: 2025
Details: The second-fastest hundred and the fastest-ever by an Indian. At just 14 years old, the young prodigy hammered a 35-ball century, ultimately scoring 101 off 38 balls to help RR chase down a target of 209 in Jaipur.

3. Yusuf Pathan | 37 Balls
Team: Rajasthan Royals
Opponent: Mumbai Indians
Year: 2010
Details: Before the Gaylestorm, Yusuf Pathan held the record for the quickest century in the competition. Walking in to bat when his team was in trouble, he bludgeoned the Mumbai Indians bowling attack, bringing up his century in 37 balls. He finished with 100 off 37 balls in a fighting effort that ended in a narrow four-run defeat.

4. Heinrich Klaasen | 37 Balls
Team: Sunrisers Hyderabad
Opponent: Kolkata Knight Riders
Year: 2025
Details: A pure power-hitting display, Klaasen smashed a 37-ball century against KKR. Known as a premier finisher, he came in during the middle overs and struck the ball cleanly to notch up his 100, finishing unbeaten on 105.

5. David Miller | 38 Balls
Team: Punjab Kings (formerly Kings XI Punjab)
Opponent: Royal Challengers Bangalore
Year: 2013
Details: Earning the nickname "Killer Miller," he rescued the Punjab Kings from a precarious position by tearing apart the bowling attack at Mohali. He reached his 100 in 38 balls, finishing on 101 not out and securing an improbable victory for his side.

6. Travis Head | 39 Balls
Team: Sunrisers Hyderabad
Opponent: Royal Challengers Bangalore
Year: 2024
Details: The aggressive Australian opener set the tone for SRH's record-breaking batting season in 2024. He brought up his century in just 39 balls, laying the foundation for Sunrisers Hyderabad to post a massive 287/3.

7. Priyansh Arya | 39 Balls
Team: Punjab Kings
Opponent: Chennai Super Kings
Year: 2025
Details: During an exceptional debut season, Priyansh Arya brought up a magnificent century against CSK in just 39 balls. The explosive knock was marked by crucial boundaries and massive sixes
