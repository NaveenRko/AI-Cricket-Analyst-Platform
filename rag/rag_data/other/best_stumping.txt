1. MS Dhoni (47 Stumpings)
Overview: The undisputed master of glovework behind the stumps, the former Chennai Super Kings (CSK) captain is widely recognized for his lightning-quick reflexes.
The Magic: Dhoni’s stumping speed often averages around \(0.08\) seconds, which is practically faster than the human eye can process. He capitalizes heavily on reading the batter's feet and sweeping off the bails the split second the batter's momentum takes them out of the crease.

2. Dinesh Karthik (37 Stumpings)
Overview: A seasoned veteran in the IPL, he played for a staggering six franchises (including Delhi, Punjab, Mumbai, Kolkata, and Bengaluru) throughout his long career.
The Magic: Karthik was known for his incredible agility and acrobatic glovework. His success came largely from his ability to cleanly gather deliveries that bounced unpredictably, instantly whipping the bails off against both pace and spin.

3. Robin Uthappa (32 Stumpings)
Overview: Although primarily recognized as a top-order batter, Uthappa stepped into wicket-keeping duties periodically, most notably for the Kolkata Knight Riders and Chennai Super Kings.
The Magic: Uthappa's high stumping count is largely due to his deep reading of the game and tactical awareness. He excelled at standing up to the stumps and catching batters off guard by relying on his natural cricketing instinct rather than just traditional wicket-keeping posture.

4. Wriddhiman Saha (26 Stumpings)
Overview: Widely considered one of the purest and most traditional wicket-keepers in Indian cricket, Saha has featured for multiple franchises, most notably Gujarat Titans and Sunrisers Hyderabad.
The Magic: His technique is textbook. Saha is exceptionally good at standing up to rapid seamers on turning tracks. His soft hands and anticipation of the ball's trajectory off the pitch allowed him to catch many batters off guard.

5. Rishabh Pant (23 Stumpings)
Overview: The dynamic Delhi Capitals stumper is known for his fearless, aggressive approach both at the crease and behind the wickets.
The Magic: Pant utilizes brute wrist strength to remove the bails. His low center of gravity and flexibility allow him to pounce on deliveries that pass the outside edge, giving him an incredibly quick "whip" action that traps batters who over-commit or lose balance
